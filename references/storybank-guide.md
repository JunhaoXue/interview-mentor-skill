# Storybank Guide

## What Is a Storybank?

A structured collection of your professional stories, indexed by competency, with STAR text, earned secrets, and strength ratings. The storybank is your interview ammunition — the better organized it is, the faster you retrieve under pressure.

## Story Structure (STAR+)

Every story follows STAR plus earned secret:

- **Situation**: Context, stakes, why it mattered
- **Task**: Your specific responsibility (not the team's)
- **Action**: What YOU did — specific steps, decisions, tradeoffs
- **Result**: Quantified outcome, what changed, ripple effects
- **Earned Secret**: The insight only you have from living this experience

## Storybank Index Format

| ID | Title | Primary Skill | Secondary Skill | Earned Secret | Strength | Use Count | Last Used |
|----|-------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|----------|-----------|-----------|

**Strength Rating (1-5):**
- 1: Raw — has potential but needs work
- 2: Developing — has specifics but missing quantification or earned secret
- 3: Solid — clear STAR, some numbers, decent earned secret
- 4: Strong — quantified, earned secret, good differentiation
- 5: Elite — unmistakably you, quantified, validated, defensible POV

## Story Details Format

For each story, maintain full text:

```markdown
#### S001 — [Title]
- Situation: [context + stakes]
- Task: [your responsibility]
- Action: [what you did — specific steps]
- Result: [quantified outcome]
- Earned Secret: [unique insight]
- Deploy for: [one-line use case]
- Version history: [date — what changed]
```

## Building Your Storybank

### Minimum Viable Storybank (5-8 stories)

Cover these core competencies:
1. **Leadership / influence** — leading without authority, driving decisions
2. **Conflict / disagreement** — handling pushback, navigating tension
3. **Failure / mistake** — what went wrong, what you learned
4. **Ambiguity / prioritization** — making decisions with incomplete info
5. **Technical challenge** — solving a hard problem
6. **Collaboration** — working across teams/functions
7. **Customer/user focus** — understanding and serving users
8. **Innovation** — doing something new or different

### Story Mining Questions

- "What's the hardest thing you've done at work?"
- "When did you disagree with your boss and what happened?"
- "What's a project that almost failed?"
- "When did you have to make a decision with incomplete data?"
- "What's something you built that you're most proud of?"
- "When did you change someone's mind about something important?"

## Rapid-Retrieval Drill

### Prerequisites
- 8+ stories in storybank

### Protocol
1. 10 rapid-fire questions, 10 seconds each
2. Candidate responds: Story ID + opening line
3. No time for full STAR — just identification and first sentence

### Scoring
- **Fast + correct**: Story matches question well, retrieved in <10s
- **Slow + correct**: Right story but took too long
- **Fast + wrong**: Quick but poor match — indexing problem
- **Blank**: No story available — gap identified

### Debrief Focus
- Which competencies had no quick answer? → Add stories
- Which question types caused hesitation? → Practice retrieval
- Did they reach for the wrong story? → Improve indexing

## Portfolio Optimization

When multiple stories compete for the same question:

### 4-Level Fit Scoring
- **Strong Fit**: Story directly addresses the question's core competency
- **Workable**: Story addresses it with some reframing
- **Stretch**: Story is tangentially related
- **Gap**: No relevant story

### Conflict Resolution (when 2+ questions want the same story)
1. Assign the story to the question where it's the strongest fit
2. For the other question, check for the next-best story
3. If no alternative exists, flag as a gap for `stories add`

## Narrative Identity

After 5+ stories, extract the 2-3 core themes:
- What patterns emerge across all stories?
- What kind of professional are you?
- What's the coherent thesis about who you are?

Every answer should subtly reinforce this identity. The goal: by interview end, the interviewer can articulate your narrative identity back to you.

## Story Maintenance

- **Refresh quarterly**: Update results with latest data
- **Retire stale stories**: If the experience is 5+ years old and you have newer examples, consider retiring
- **Track usage**: Stories used 3+ times in real interviews should be evaluated — are they still landing?
- **Version history**: When you improve a story, note what changed and when
