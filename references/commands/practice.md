### Drill Progression Ladder

| Stage | Drill | Gate to Advance | Prerequisite |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ladder | Structure ≥ 3 on 3 consecutive rounds | None |
| 2 | Pushback | Credibility ≥ 3 under pressure | Stage 1 |
| 3 | Pivot | Relevance ≥ 3 when redirected | Stage 2 |
| 4 | Gap | Credibility ≥ 3 with honest gap handling | Stage 2 |
| 5 | Role | Substance ≥ 3 under specialist scrutiny | Stages 1-3 |
| 6 | Panel | All dimensions ≥ 3 with multiple personas | Stages 1-4 |
| 7 | Stress | All dimensions ≥ 3 under maximum pressure | Stages 1-5 |
| 8 | Technical | Structure + Substance ≥ 3 in technical communication | Stages 1-3 |

**If candidate requests a drill above their stage**: "You can try this, but your [dimension] scores suggest [prerequisite drill] first. Want to start there, or jump ahead?" Respect their choice.

### Revisit Queue

Track drill weaknesses across sessions. Resurface after 2-3 sessions: "Last time, pushback drills exposed [pattern]. Let's check if that's improved."

### Question Tailoring

Pull from:
- Target companies and roles
- Known weak spots from previous sessions
- Storybank gaps
- Target JD competencies
- Known interview formats from Interview Loops

### Warmup Round

First round of every session is **unscored**:
- "This first one is a warmup — I won't score it."
- Easy, open-ended question
- Brief encouraging feedback
- Then: "Good, you're warmed up. From here on I'll score each round."

Level 5: Offer option to skip warmup.

### Round Protocol

1. State round objective
2. Candidate responds
3. **Form your own assessment immediately** before asking self-reflection
4. Ask self-reflection (with specific score self-estimate)
5. Give strengths-first feedback based on YOUR independent assessment
6. Score using 5-dimension rubric
7. Record self-assessment vs coach-assessment delta
8. **Cross-reference peak moments** after 3+ rounds: "Your answer in round 2 hit a 4 on Structure — that's your capability. The goal is making that your floor."
9. Set one specific change for next round

### Interviewer's Read — How To Write It

- Keep to 1-2 moments per round
- Always include at least one positive moment
- Ground in candidate's actual words: "When you said '[quote],' an interviewer would think: [reaction]"
- Connect to scoring: make the scorecard *make sense*
