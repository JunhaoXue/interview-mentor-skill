# Differentiation Protocol

## Why Differentiation Matters

Differentiation is what separates "qualified" from "memorable." In competitive processes, the gap between Hire and Strong Hire is almost always Differentiation. The biggest enemy: AI-polished prep that sounds "correct" but could be anyone's.

## Earned Secrets

An earned secret is an insight that comes from direct experience — something you know because you lived it, not because you read it.

### How to Extract

Five questions (use in `stories add` and `stories improve`):

1. **"What surprised you most about this experience?"** — Surprises reveal where reality differed from expectation. That gap IS the earned secret.

2. **"What would you do differently now, and why?"** — Hindsight reveals judgment. The "why" is where the insight lives.

3. **"What do you know from this that most people in your role don't know?"** — Forces the candidate to articulate their unique perspective.

4. **"What's the counterintuitive thing about this situation?"** — Counterintuitive insights are naturally differentiating.

5. **"If you were advising someone facing the same situation, what would you tell them that they wouldn't find in any book?"** — Separates lived wisdom from textbook knowledge.

### Good vs Bad Earned Secrets

**Good** (specific, experiential, defensible):
- "I learned that the fastest way to kill a feature is to let it succeed at small scale — it becomes politically unkillable before you have real data."
- "After three failed launches, I realized that engineering velocity is a vanity metric — the teams that ship fast but don't measure are slower than teams that measure first."

**Bad** (generic, could be anyone's):
- "I learned that communication is important."
- "I realized that data-driven decisions are better."
- "I discovered that teamwork matters."

## Spiky POVs

A spiky POV is a defensible, surprising stance backed by experience. It's "spiky" because not everyone would agree — and that's the point.

### Characteristics
- **Defensible**: You can back it up with evidence from your experience
- **Surprising**: It goes against conventional wisdom or common advice
- **Authentic**: It comes from your actual experience, not manufactured controversy
- **Specific**: It applies to a concrete domain, not vague platitudes

### How to Develop

1. Start from an earned secret
2. Generalize it into a principle
3. Test: "Would a smart, experienced person disagree with this?" If yes, it's spiky enough.
4. Practice defending it under pushback (use `practice pushback`)

### Examples by Role

**Product Manager**: "I believe the best PMs should be the ones most willing to kill their own features. Most PMs optimize for shipping; the great ones optimize for pruning."

**Engineer**: "I've found that the most productive engineering teams I've been on had the slowest release cycles. Speed comes from fewer, more deliberate changes — not from moving fast."

**Designer**: "I think user research is overrated for 0-to-1 products. Every breakthrough product I've worked on started with a strong opinion, not a survey."

**Data Scientist**: "The models that had the most business impact in my career were the simplest ones. Complexity in modeling is usually a sign that you haven't understood the problem yet."

## Clarity Under Pressure

Differentiation is hardest to maintain under stress. When nervous, candidates default to safe, generic answers.

### Drills
1. **Constraint Ladder**: Tell the same story at 30s, 60s, 90s, 3min. The earned secret should survive every version.
2. **Pushback Drill**: After delivering your spiky POV, face: "I disagree — isn't that just [conventional wisdom]?" Practice holding your ground with evidence.
3. **Interruption Drill**: Start a story, get interrupted, pivot to a different question. Can you still land the earned secret?

## Integration Points

- **stories**: Every story should have an earned secret before it's "complete"
- **analyze**: Flag answers scoring < 3 on Differentiation for earned secret extraction
- **practice**: Test earned secrets under pressure
- **mock**: Track whether differentiation holds across a full interview arc
- **prep**: Map earned secrets to predicted questions
